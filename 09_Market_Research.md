### The Soiling Problem
- **Dust accumulation** in North India is among the highest globally due to:
  - Proximity to Thar Desert (dust transport)
  - High construction activity (Faridabad-Gurgaon corridor)
  - Industrial emissions (Faridabad is an industrial hub)
  - Agricultural burning (seasonal — October-November)
  - High AQI levels (Faridabad regularly 200-400 AQI)
- **Soiling rate in NCR**: 0.3-0.8% efficiency loss per day without cleaning
- **After 30 days without cleaning**: 9-24% output loss
- **After 90 days without cleaning**: 25-40% output loss in dusty seasons
- **Post-monsoon soiling spike**: Mud formation from rain + dust, harder to clean

> **Source**: NREL soiling studies, IIT Delhi solar research papers, industry reports

### Faridabad Solar Adoption Drivers
1. **Rising electricity costs**: DHBVN tariffs have increased 5-8% annually. Solar payback now 3-5 years vs 6-7 years earlier.
2. **Government subsidy**: PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana — 40% subsidy on 3 kW systems. Extended in 2025-26 budget.
3. **Net metering policy**: Haryana net metering allows exporting surplus — financial incentive to maximize generation.
4. **Builder integration**: New projects in Greater Faridabad (Sector 75-89) often include pre-installed solar.
5. **Industrial cost pressure**: Faridabad's manufacturing units (steel, auto parts, textiles) face high power costs — solar ROI drives adoption.
6. **Status symbol**: In premium colonies (Greenfields, Charmwood), solar panels are a visible sign of eco-conscious affluence.

## 4. Customer Pain Points & Insights

### Primary Research (Simulated — Adjust with Actual Local Surveys)

**Pain Points Identified**:
1. **"Didn't know cleaning was needed"** — 50% of solar owners are unaware that panels need professional cleaning. They assume rain handles it.
2. **"Don't know who to call"** — No known branded cleaning service. Have to rely on local labor with no accountability.
3. **"Worried about damage"** — Fear of scratches, broken panels, or roof leaks from inexperienced cleaners.
4. **"No time to supervise"** — Working professionals can't be home to watch the cleaner.
5. **"Can't tell if it was done well"** — No proof of work. No way to measure cleaning effectiveness.
6. **"Seasonal inconsistency"** — Clean once, then forget for a year. No reminder system.
7. **"Price uncertainty"** — No standard rates. Local cleaners quote random amounts.
8. **"Safety concern"** — Worried about liability if worker falls from roof.

## 5. Market Trends & Future Outlook

### Trends Favoring the Business
1. **Solar adoption accelerating**: Haryana's solar policy targets + PM Surya Ghar scheme = 2-3x growth in rooftop solar by 2030.
2. **"Servitization" of home services**: Urban India is increasingly comfortable with app-based home services (Urban Company, Housejoy). Solar cleaning is the next frontier.
3. **Insurtech integration**: Solar insurers are beginning to require proof of maintenance for claims. Professional cleaning reports = compliance.
4. **Water scarcity awareness**: Faridabad faces water shortages. Water-efficient/waterless cleaning is a strong differentiator.
5. **Corporate ESG mandates**: Companies with solar installations need documented O&M for sustainability reports. AMC with thermal imaging reports fills this need.
6. **IoT & smart solar**: Next-gen inverters have WiFi monitoring. Customers checking their app daily will notice generation dips faster — driving demand for cleaning.

### Threats
1. **DIY cleaning kits improving**: Better consumer-grade tools may reduce professional demand for highly price-sensitive segment.
2. **Robotic cleaners becoming affordable**: Solar cleaning robots (currently ₹3-7 lakhs) may become accessible at ₹1 lakh by 2030. Could disrupt labor-based model.
3. **Solar installers bundling free cleaning**: If installers start offering free cleaning for 5 years to close sales, aftermarket cleaning demand shrinks.
4. **Water regulation**: If Faridabad imposes restrictions on water use for cleaning, need to pivot to waterless tech faster.
5. **Economic downturn**: Solar cleaning is discretionary. In a recession, homeowners may DIY or skip cleaning.

## 7. Regulatory & Policy Landscape

### Haryana Solar Policy Highlights
- **Haryana Solar Power Policy 2021 (amended 2024)**:
  - Net metering for systems up to 500 kW
  - 40% central subsidy under PM Surya Ghar for 1-3 kW residential systems
  - Additional state subsidy for specific categories
  - Mandatory solar for new buildings >500 sq yards (applicable in certain urban areas)
  - No specific regulation on solar panel cleaning (unregulated — opportunity to set standards)

### Relevant Government Bodies
- **HAREDA** (Haryana Renewable Energy Development Agency): State nodal agency for solar. Potential certification partner.
- **MNRE** (Ministry of New & Renewable Energy): Central policy. National Solar Mission phase targets.
- **DISCOM (DHBVN)**: Electricity distribution — net metering approvals, billing. Understanding net metering helps in customer conversations.
- **Faridabad Municipal Corporation**: Local business licenses, water usage permissions if needed.

### Potential Government Tenders/Contracts
- **Government building solar maintenance**: All govt buildings with solar need O&M. SuryaClean can bid for these.
- **Smart City Faridabad projects**: Solar installations in smart city infrastructure.
- **Haryana Solar Park O&M**: Future opportunity when parks are developed.
